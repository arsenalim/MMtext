How do digital footprints left on the Internet affect personal freedom of digital expression?

People should aim to limit their own freedom of expression online in order to stay safe and avoid serious consequences. Not taking care of your digital footprint can lead to serious problems later in life

Start reading

What is a freedom of digital expression?

People tend not to think about the consequences. They usually think they know what is going to happen and how to deal with it, so there is no need to worry about it. But it is really hard to predict the future accurately. Sometimes consequences can be hidden and long-lasting. It can affect your life years after you've forgotten what caused it. That's why people should try to limit their own freedom of expression, especially on the internet, in order to be safe and avoid serious consequences.


Freedom of digital expression is the ability to post anything on the internet. It can guarantee that you are free to share your opinions and post any information you want. But your freedom of digital expression can be limited because other people can track what you do and some hackers can steal your online persona. Anything you have said in the past can affect what you can say in the future and how other users will react to your posts. In some situations, your freedom of expression can be restricted or removed, and then it is no longer a freedom. It is important to be careful about your posts, comments and anything else you leave on the internet to avoid unpleasant consequences. As Internet users say:

"The Internet remembers everything".

So it is impossible to erase your digital footprint, and people should take care of what their digital footprint looks like, because it stays forever.

What are the negative consequences?



Intrusion and manipulation

The digital footprint can lead to personal information that can be stolen by hackers or companies. Your digital footprint is not only what you post, it could also be some metadata that different sites can collect themselves without asking your permission.

Data such as

  • your IP address
  • location
  • passwords
  • emails
  • even the model of your computer

can be easily stolen. There are many examples of personal data being leaked from large websites and companies such as Facebook or Amazon. This is why there are large databases of personal information for millions of people on the Dark Web. When your information is leaked, it is easier to manipulate you. Using your personal information found online, hackers can threaten by

  • posting your personal information on different sources where you wouldn't want it
  • steal your online money
  • chat with your friends using your account
  • steal your online personality to do illegal things in your name

It is a restriction of your digital freedom, because now you will do what they want, but not you, because now some hackers can easily destroy your life. It is not only a digital restriction, but also a physical one. It can affect you in the real world.



Public reaction

You may have posted something provocative in the past, and it can determine how people feel about you in the present. Cancellation culture is a growing phenomenon these days and you can be a victim of it. Many years ago the internet was free and you could post anything you wanted and it would not be considered serious, just a joke. But now it can be taken as your real opinion and you can't justify it. This puts a lot of pressure on your online personality, restricting what you can say and changing the way people view you. For example, Elon Mask gets banned every week because he always posts something really provocative and controversial. Now he has too many haters for a mortal. But he is too powerful and too rich to face any serious consequences. But many people aren't. There are many other examples of actors and singers who have been cancelled and lost their jobs and all their contracts. So people who have been fired can't really be active influencers on the internet because it's almost impossible to restore their reputation. After someone has found something inappropriate in their digital footprint, the only thing these people will get is hate. That's to say, all their opinions are now banned and they lose their ability to speak freely on the internet.

Companies

According to the statistics, it is not only society and hackers that can restrict freedom of expression on the internet, but also companies. Many platforms such as Facebook or Google don't want to see unpleasant content hosted on their site, so they have to regulate content, thereby limiting freedom of expression. It is not about your digital footprint and what you have done in the past, but sometimes you can be banned by a service, not a user or hacker. For example, if you have broken YouTube's rules several times, you may lose the ability to post videos there.

But...

On the other hand, you may not be a worthwhile target to be leaked or hacked, maybe no one on the internet is interested in your digital footprint. It is really difficult and wasteful to hack and steal some information on the internet, the process of hacking is really hard and complicated, so the information to be leaked should be worthwhile. It can be some personal information or provocative posts of some celebrities, and if you are not a popular person, you can not worry about it. But your money is what you should be worried about. If you have large sums of money in your bank account, it is likely that you will be hacked by someone who wants to steal your money. Most people on the internet are not really rich and famous people. The truth is, you are probably not an interesting victim of leakage, so you can do whatever you want with your digital footprint. According to stationx.net, 1.5 billion Facebook accounts were stolen in 2021. That is almost half of all accounts. That means someone in your family or circle of friends has probably been hacked. But you don't know such examples, do you? That's because you're not a worthwhile target for hackers. This means that if you are an average internet user, you should not worry about your digital footprint. The chance of leaked data somehow affecting the freedom of digital expression of the average user is really small.
